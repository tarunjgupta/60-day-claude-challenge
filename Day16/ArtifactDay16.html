Where Each Leads

Competitive Advantages

Scorecard — Key Fundamental Dimensions

🟢 Where NVIDIA leads

  • Scale and profitability: ~7× AMD's revenue, with gross/operating/net margins roughly 20–50 points higher across the board.
  • Capital efficiency: ROE of 114% and ROIC of 105% vs AMD's single digits, on an almost identical (low) debt load.
  • Cash generation: $119B TTM free cash flow vs AMD's $8.6B — over 13× larger.
  • Cheaper relative to its own growth: trailing P/E sits well below its 5-year average and PEG is under 0.5.
  • Only one of the two pays a dividend, however small.

🔴 Where AMD leads

  • Stock momentum: +337% over the past 52 weeks vs NVIDIA's +47%, reflecting a sharper re-rating off a smaller base.
  • Lower price/book multiple (10.9× vs 27.6×), and a PEG closer to 1.0 (fairly priced for growth) vs NVIDIA's sub-0.5 PEG (arguably underpriced for growth, or reflecting higher embedded risk).
  • More diversified revenue mix — Data Center is roughly half of AMD's business versus close to ~90% concentration at NVIDIA, which some investors view as a lower single-segment risk profile.
  • Fastest-growing major partnership pipeline among NVIDIA's challengers: large multi-GW AI accelerator deployment commitments from Meta and expanding hyperscaler adoption of EPYC server CPUs.
Neutral summary: Both companies are direct beneficiaries of AI infrastructure spending but occupy different positions in the cycle. NVIDIA is the larger, far more profitable incumbent with dominant data-center share, a near debt-free balance sheet, and valuation multiples that — on a trailing basis — sit below its own historical average. AMD is the smaller, faster-growing challenger whose share price has rallied much further over the past year on the back of recent design wins and hyperscaler partnerships, but whose GAAP earnings and margins remain well behind NVIDIA's, which is why its trailing P/E looks stretched even as its forward multiple is more moderate. Neither comparison points to one being definitively "better" — they represent different risk/return trade-offs within the same AI compute theme.

Risk Factors

Key Risks to Monitor

NVIDIA

  • Heavy revenue concentration in Data Center/AI compute (~90% of sales) makes results highly sensitive to any slowdown in hyperscaler AI capex.
  • Valuation, while moderate versus its own history, is still a premium multiple that assumes continued exceptional growth.
  • Export-control and geopolitical risk around advanced chip sales to certain markets, and reliance on TSMC for advanced-node manufacturing capacity.
  • A small number of large cloud customers account for an outsized share of revenue — customer concentration risk.

AMD

  • Trailing valuation multiples are very stretched relative to current GAAP earnings; any growth disappointment could compress the multiple sharply given how far the stock has already run.
  • Management has flagged TSMC's advanced-node (CoWoS) capacity as a potential bottleneck that could limit 2026 AI chip shipments versus demand.
  • No dividend — total return depends entirely on continued share-price appreciation.
  • Still significantly smaller scale than NVIDIA in AI data-center compute, competing against an incumbent with a much larger cash and R&D base.
