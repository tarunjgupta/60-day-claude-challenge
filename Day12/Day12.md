## 🧠 Key Learnings

### 1. ATS Optimization
- ATS systems parse resumes and cover letters for exact keyword matches
- Using job description language verbatim (e.g., "microservices architecture", "prompt engineering", "transfer learning") significantly improves ATS pass rates
- Quantified achievements (500+ DSA problems, 6-class classification, 200K+ star repo) beat vague claims every time

### 2. Recruiter Outreach Strategy
- Recruiters spend ~6–8 seconds on cold emails — lead with the single most impressive credential
- Two separate emails (recruiter vs. hiring manager) perform better than one generic application email
- A published research paper as the opening hook is rare at fresher level — lead with it always

### 3. Personal Branding
- Your resume lists *what you did*. Your personal brand explains *why it matters*
- Positioning statement format: **[Role] with [proof point] who [unique differentiator]**
- LinkedIn headline is the highest-ROI single change you can make before applying

### 4. Career Strategy
- Skill gap analysis revealed clear next steps: AWS SageMaker, LLM fine-tuning (LoRA/QLoRA), MLflow, and vector databases
- A 90-day structured roadmap beats vague "I should learn X" intentions
- Interview talking points should be prepared as stories, not bullet facts
